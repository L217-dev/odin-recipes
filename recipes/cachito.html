Cachito

Description

The cachito is a soft, savory bread from Venezuela, traditionally filled with ham and, in some variations, cheese or bacon. Its dough has a slight sweetness and is baked until golden and fluffy. It is a popular choice for breakfast or a snack, often enjoyed with coffee or juice.

This bread is the Venezuelan version of stuffed croissants and is commonly found in bakeries across the country. The balance between the dough's sweetness and the savory filling makes it a favorite for many. Homemade versions allow for customization with different ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups of flour
  • 2 teaspoons of dry yeast
  • ¼ cup of sugar
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of warm milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons of butter, softened
  • 200g of ham (or a mix of ham, cheese, or bacon)
  • 1 egg yolk (for brushing)
